Who It's For

03 Zapier + AI Automation

Three Live Zapier Automations

Why I built it → I hit a point where I thought: I should not need to manually drag every single thing across the finish line. So I stopped.

Three no-code AI automations built and running in Zapier — a welcome workflow, a health risk alert, and a branching churn analyzer with two conditional paths. Each uses OpenAI to generate context-aware output and delivers it automatically. The most advanced includes a 10-step branching workflow with a Google Sheets write-back loop.

Automation A — Welcome Workflow
What Triggers It
A new customer row is added to Google Sheets — fires automatically the moment a new account is created, no manual step required.
What It Does
OpenAI generates a personalized 30-day onboarding plan using the customer's company name, industry, and plan tier — then Gmail delivers it to the assigned CSM instantly.
Why It Matters
Eliminates the generic copy-paste onboarding template. Every new customer gets a plan written specifically for their business type — before the CSM has even opened their laptop.
Welcome Automation — Actual Zapier Workflow
Step 1
Google Sheets · Trigger
AI Automated Workflow — New Row
Fires when a new customer row is added to Google Sheets. Pulls company data, plan tier, contact info, and assigned CSM to feed into the AI generation step.
Step 2
ChatGPT (OpenAI) · Conversation
Generate Personalized Onboarding Plan
OpenAI receives the customer data and generates a structured, personalized 30-day onboarding plan — Week 1 priorities, milestone targets, top features to drive, and a Day 7 check-in agenda tailored to the customer's industry and tier.
Step 3
Filter by Zapier · Conditions
Filter Conditions — Quality Gate
Only proceeds if the AI output meets defined conditions — ensuring the email only fires when the onboarding plan is complete and valid. Prevents partial or empty outputs from reaching the CSM's inbox.
Step 4
Gmail · Send Email
Deliver Plan to Assigned CSM
The complete, validated onboarding plan is emailed to the assigned CSM automatically — ready to send or customize before the first customer touchpoint.

Automation B — Health Risk Alert
What Triggers It
A change in Google Sheets flags an account with health risk data — usage drops, engagement gaps, or manual risk flags entered by the CSM trigger the automation immediately.
What Makes It Advanced
Uses two OpenAI steps — Step 2 analyzes the raw account data for risk signals, Step 3 generates a tailored intervention prompt based on that analysis. Two-pass AI reasoning, not a single generic prompt.
Why It Matters
Churn rarely announces itself. This automation catches accounts going quiet and gets an AI-generated action plan into the CSM's inbox before the account ever raises their hand — turning reactive into proactive.
Health Risk Automation — Actual Zapier Workflow
Step 1
Google Sheets · Trigger
Health Risk Automation — Row Updated
Fires when account health data is updated in Google Sheets — health score changes, usage drops, or CSM-flagged risk entries trigger the Zap instantly.
Step 2
ChatGPT (OpenAI) · Analyze Text
Analyze Account for Risk Signals
First OpenAI pass: reads raw account data and identifies what specific risk signals are present — usage drop, stakeholder gap, support friction, AI feature abandonment. Outputs a structured risk analysis.
Step 3
ChatGPT (OpenAI) · Send Prompt
Generate Tailored Intervention Plan
Second OpenAI pass: takes the risk analysis from Step 2 and generates a specific intervention plan — who to contact, what to say, what outcome to aim for, and when to escalate.
Step 4
Gmail · Send Email
Alert CSM with Action Plan
Delivers the full risk assessment and intervention plan directly to the CSM's inbox — triggered automatically, no manual review step required before the alert fires.
Live Zapier Canvas — Health Risk Automation
Actual Zapier canvas — Health Risk Automation v4 · Google Sheets → ChatGPT (×2) → Gmail
Automation C — Churn Risk Analyzer (Advanced)
What Makes This Advanced
Uses Paths by Zapier to split into two parallel branches based on conditions — each path runs its own AI prompt, sends a different email, and writes results back to Google Sheets. A true conditional workflow, not a linear chain.
How the Branching Works
Step 2 evaluates the account data and splits: one path handles high-risk accounts with an urgent intervention prompt, the other handles at-risk accounts with a proactive outreach prompt. Each branch delivers a different AI-generated alert.
The Write-Back Loop
Both paths end with a Google Sheets update — automatically logging that the alert was sent, which path fired, and when. Creates a live audit trail of every churn risk intervention without any manual logging.
Churn Risk Analyzer — Actual Zapier Workflow (10 Steps)

Evaluates the account data and routes to the correct branch. Each path has its own conditions — account severity, risk tier, or specific signal type — determining which AI prompt and alert fires.
Steps 3–6

Path A — High-Risk Intervention
Path conditions confirm account meets high-risk threshold → ChatGPT generates an urgent intervention plan → Gmail delivers it to the CSM → Google Sheets logs the alert with timestamp and path fired.
Steps 7–10
Path B · Conditions → ChatGPT → Gmail → Sheets
Path B — At-Risk Proactive Outreach
Path conditions confirm account is at-risk but not critical → ChatGPT generates a proactive re-engagement prompt → Gmail delivers it → Google Sheets updates the row, closing the audit loop automatically.

04 Custom GPT

Voice of Customer Synthesis GPT

Why I built it → Customers were telling us things. Those things were living in tickets, notes, and my memory. Only one of those is scalable.

Paste in raw customer feedback — support tickets, call notes, survey responses, anything. Get back a structured VoC report with top themes, sentiment breakdown, product gaps, and prioritized internal recommendations ready to bring to Product.

The Problem It Solves
CSMs collect enormous amounts of customer signal — but it lives in tickets, notes, and call summaries scattered across tools. Synthesizing it into something Product can actually act on takes hours and often gets skipped. This GPT makes VoC reporting a 5-minute habit instead of a monthly project.
Why It's on This Portfolio
Kyana's resume specifically calls out "synthesizing feedback, friction points, and behavioral trends into prioritized input for Product and cross-functional teams." This GPT makes that skill tangible, demonstrable, and repeatable.
Input Accepted
Support ticket summaries · Call notes or transcripts · NPS / CSAT survey responses · Slack or email threads from customers · Any mix of the above — the GPT handles unstructured input
Output Delivered
5-section VoC report: Sentiment breakdown · Top themes ranked by frequency · Product gaps with supporting evidence · Prioritized internal recommendations · What's landing (retention anchors)
Sample Input — Mixed Raw Feedback

Ticket 1: User can't figure out how to export reports to PDF. Asked support 3x.

Ticket 2: Billing portal keeps logging them out mid-session. Very frustrating.

Call note: Really happy with the dispatch module — said it saved them 2hrs/day. Wants a better mobile experience though.

NPS (score 7): "Product is solid but the reporting is clunky. I always have to call someone to get the data I actually want."

Call note: Asked why there's no bulk editing for work orders. Team wastes time doing it one by one.

Survey: "Love the team, the software needs work on the UI side."

Sample Output

Sentiment: Positive 33% · Neutral 17% · Frustrated 50%

Top Theme 1: Reporting friction — 3 of 6 items. Customers can't self-serve the data they need without contacting support. Priority: HIGH.

Top Theme 2: UI / workflow efficiency — 2 of 6. Bulk editing and mobile gaps slowing daily operations. Priority: HIGH.

Product Gap: PDF export — no native path. Customer impact: repeated support contacts, erodes self-sufficiency.

Recommendation 1: Escalate PDF export + reporting UX to Product with frequency data. Owner: CSM to Product. Urgency: This Quarter.
